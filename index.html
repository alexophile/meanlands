Welcome to the Meanlands!

This is a West Marches Style campaign using the Dungeons and Dragons 5th Edition ruleset and a mix of official and homebrew content.


What do all those things mean?

West Marches means that this campaign is:
1) Large Group: We can support 20-some-odd people playing in the same persistent world.

2) Open Table: Your party for a single adventure can consist of anyone in the campaign.

3) Player Guided: A session occurs when enough players decide on something they’re interested in and schedule a time with a dm to go check it out.

Why is that cool?

For one, it’s a much more versatile play style, especially fitting for people who can’t commit to a weekly game but still want to play on occasion. You also get the opportunity to participate in the world (through the forum and discord) when you’re not able to play.

In game, this also means you can make progress on multiple goals at once in collaboration or even sometimes in competition when there’s a disagreement on world-changing decisions. Time moves at a steady pace, new towns, strongholds, religions, and factions can rise and fall. There will be generations of adventurers living in the world forged by the decisions of their predecessors.

At the bottom of it all, though, we just want to play D&D in a fun, friendly world that everyone gets to contribute to. You can come and go, characters can charge valiantly to certain doom without disrupting the narrative, because the narrative is whatever you decide.

The World

The Town of Whystia

As of right now, the town of Whystia is nearly empty. There’s an inn, a blacksmith, and a couple of scattered friendly faces. But not much in the way of infrastructure. The materials you gather in downtime are spent making the town what it is to be.  

As you build new buildings, add-ons, and upgrades, you’ll gain access to better equipment and services and attract new and interesting townsfolk.

Artifacts

There are also Artifacts of great power strewn about the land that can stretch the limits of the existing world, adding new races, classes, spells, technology, or even changing the face of the world entirely.

The Map

One of the key features of a West Marches style campaign is that the players are collectively responsible for aggregating their shared understanding of the world. Part of this is done through adventure logs, and part through a shared map that players will get to craft together.

Doing Stuff

Going on Adventures

An adventure happens when players organize themselves to go investigate a particular location or event and get a dm to agree and schedule a time. There are no automatic weekly sessions or anything. After an adventure, every party member is encouraged to write up their account of what happened. Such efforts will be rewarded with lore points that can be used to reveal secrets about the world.

As the Meanlands are both treacherous and largely uncharted, sometimes you’ll need a little help getting back to town. Maybe you’ve overstayed your welcome in the wilderness, maybe you’re a bit lost, or maybe you just want to use all the time you can to press deep into the forest. Lucky for you, Oswald Bristlefeather runs a little establishment called The Landing Pad and offers, free of charge, one of his custom teleport scrolls to every adventurer that heads out into the wilderness. But as with all Dohwar deals, there’s a catch: these scrolls will drop you into the basement of The Landing Pad which happens to be a powerful magical prison and he’ll request a nominal processing fee for your release.

Spending Downtime

Every week that passes in real life passes a month in game time. In addition to adventuring, you can spend 7 days worth of downtime doing a variety of activities including harvesting wood, mining stone and ore, learning new skills, crafting items, and pitching in to help build the town up.

Lore Points

Anyone who logs a writeup of their adventure will receive a Lore Point from Loremaster Saal’zan. These points can be spent on blessings from the Church of Incremental Advancement which will grant you bonuses to your downtime activities.

Creating A Character

Allowed Races:

  • Player's Handbook
  • Sword Coast Adventurer's Guide
  • Elemental Evil Player's Companion
  • Volo's Guide to Monsters
  • The Tortle Package
  • Mordenkainen's Tome of Foes
  • Races with a flying speed (Aarakocra and Winged Tiefling)
  • are allowed, but gain the ability to fly only at lvl 5;
  • No monstrous races:
  • (Bugbear, Goblin, Hobgoblin, Kobold, Orc, Yuan-ti)
  • No Unearthed Arcana races

Allowed Classes:

  • Player’s Handbook
  • Sword Coast Adventurer's Guide
  • Xanathar’s Guide to Everything

* If any ability states that "Your group can't become lost except by magical means.", instead it will read "You gain advantage on Wisdom (Survival) checks for navigation."


When you’re done, add your character (http://www.meanlands.com/newcharacter) and you’re ready to go.

And that’s it! Jump in the Discord with any questions and to get started hearing about rumors around town.
